Validate Results

Before any dataset reaches the public catalog, it passes through the dataset validator: an automated check that verifies schema, spatial and temporal coverage, units, and missing-data ratios so every contribution stays consistent and reusable.

The same rigor applies to models. Forecasts are scored against shared baselines with standard nowcasting metrics — POD, FAR, CSI, and RMSE — across lead times and extreme-rainfall cases, so performance claims are reproducible and comparable.

Reviewing these checks, spotting edge cases, and improving the validator keeps the project on a stronger, more trustworthy foundation. Your critical eye helps the community understand what truly works and what needs improvement.

Support the Community

MLCast grows on shared effort and shared resources. There are many ways to keep the project moving forward.

Donate GPU hours so we can train, tune, and benchmark larger nowcasting models the community can reuse.

Improve the documentation with guides, examples, and clear explanations that lower the barrier for new contributors.

Help spread the word to bring in more datasets, ideas, and people who can push the project further.
