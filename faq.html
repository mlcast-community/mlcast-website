Questions & Answers.

Everything you need to know about MLCast, weather nowcasting, and how to get involved in the community.

What is MLCast?

expand_more

MLCast is an open-source community building shared datasets, software, and machine-learning models for weather nowcasting. It started as a grassroots effort among researchers in September 2024 and has since also become the official Working Group 6 on Nowcasting in the EUMETNET E-AI Optional Programme — while staying open to anyone, anywhere. The community and its code live at github.com/mlcast-community.

What doesn't MLCast do?

expand_more

MLCast is intentionally scoped:

  • No operational forecasts or real-time data delivery — MLCast is a research and development community for operational tools, not a forecasting or data service.
  • Observation-to-observation only — the focus is high-resolution predictions (sometimes called DOP, direct observation prediction), not full numerical weather prediction.
  • Short-range only — the focus is nowcasting (0–6h lead times), extending at most to sub-daily horizons. No medium-range, (sub)seasonal, or climate forecasting; for that, see our friends at anemoi at ECMWF.
  • Machine-learning methods only — for classical, non-ML nowcasting techniques, see our friends at the pysteps community.

Why weather nowcasting?

expand_more

Weather nowcasting focuses on predicting weather conditions over the next few minutes to several hours. These predictions are vital for tracking severe weather events and complement traditional forecasting approaches.

How do I get started?

expand_more

Join the conversation on Slack (try #start-here), or browse the repositories at github.com/mlcast-community. Our Contributing page lays out concrete paths — sharing data, writing code, training models, validating results — so you can pick whatever matches your skills.

How can I contribute?

expand_more

Contributions include sharing datasets, writing code, training models, validating results, improving documentation, and supporting the community — see the paths laid out on our Contributing page. All experience levels are welcome.

Do I need a GPU?

expand_more

GPUs aren't required for many contributions, like documentation, testing, dataset validation, and data work. Training and benchmarking models does need GPU access, but not your own — MLCast pools shared compute, including allocations on CINECA's Leonardo HPC, that contributors can use.

How do I train a model?

expand_more

Install mlcast and mlcast-datasets, pull or sample a dataset from the catalog, configure your experiment (architecture, loss, sampler), and launch training through the CLI. The Software page walks through each step with copyable commands.

Where does the data come from?

expand_more

Data comes from national meteorological services, hosted on the European Weather Cloud — currently five countries and growing, see the live Dataset Catalog for the full list. Satellite imagery (including Meteosat Third Generation products) is in progress too. Every dataset is converted to a common Zarr format and validated before being added to the catalog.

How are models evaluated?

expand_more

Models are evaluated on held-out validation and test data using standard machine-learning and meteorological verification metrics, so results stay comparable across architectures trained on the same datasets. A shared benchmarking suite (mlcast-benchmarks) is in development to standardize this further across the community.

How do I use a pretrained model?

expand_more

The mlcast package itself is still pre-1.0 — a ConvGRU baseline is landing now, with a latent-diffusion architecture at an early proof-of-concept stage — so packaged pretrained weights for one-line inference aren't there yet. In the meantime, several architectures used within the community (DGMR, LDCast, GPTCast) already have pretrained weights in their own repositories; check the links on the Software page or ask on Slack for the current best starting point.

Where can I ask for help?
